History of University Learning Centers

  • 2007

    Under the name STEM Mentors (and, later, STEM Club), we place enthusiastic college tutors and mentors in elementary, middle, and high schools in Central Indiana. They help students achieve excellent grades in STEM studies and encourage students to focus their postsecondary studies in STEM fields.

  • 2011

    We change our name to Indy Learning Centers to indicate our addition of languages and the arts to our STEM offerings.

  • 2013

    Indy Learning Centers implements data tracking and online learning.

  • 2016

    University Learning Centers Foundation launches.

  • 2017

    University Learning Centers Benefit Corporation launches.



History Detail of University Learning Centers

2007-2008 Academic Year (AY): Create the Indy Learning Centers program STEM Mentors

  • Work with 4 schools and 16 tutors in after-school programs
  • Host Pathways to Engineering Camp at Indiana University-Purdue University-Indianapolis (IUPUI)

2008-2009 AY: Renew funding and grow program

  • Work with 6 schools and 24 tutors in after-school programs
  • Host Pathways to Engineering Camp at IUPUI
  • Partner with Indiana Campus Compact and AmeriCorps

2009-2010 AY: Add additional partnerships as STEM Club

  • Work with 11 schools and 28 tutors in after-school programs
  • Partner with Learning Center Success LLC
  • Serve Indianapolis Public Schools (IPS) secondary schools
  • Partner with Employ Indy Youth Works
  • Partner with INSTEMWorks project
  • Host Pathways to Engineering Camp at IUPUI
  • Partner with Indiana Campus Compact and AmeriCorps

2010-2011 AY: Rebrand as Indy Learning Centers

  • Work with IPS secondary schools - 12 schools and 50 tutors
  • Work with MSDWT - 11 schools and 28 tutors
  • Work with Learning Center Success LLC
  • Partner with Indiana Campus Compact and AmeriCorps
  • Host Pathways to Engineering Camp at IUPUI

2011-2012 AY: Focus on strengths

  • Partner to serve students at George Washington Community School (funding provided by the Marry Rigg Foundation)
  • Partner with Indiana Campus Compact Know How to Go grant
  • Work with IPS secondary schools - 12 schools and 50 tutors
  • Work with Learning Center Success LLC
  • Host Pathways to Engineering Camp at IUPUI

2012-2013 AY: Develop online resources

  • Work with IPS secondary schools - 12 schools and 50 tutors
  • Work with Learning Center Success LLC
  • Partner with Indiana Campus Compact Faculty Fellows Program
  • Work with George Washington Community School (funds provided by the Marry Rigg Foundation)

2013-2014 AY: Invest in our program

2014-2015 AY: Expand to other districts
